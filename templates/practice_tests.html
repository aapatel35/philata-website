Listening Practice

4 Sections
Section 1: Everyday Conversation Listening

A conversation between two people in an everyday social context. Practice completing forms, notes, and tables.

Sample: Hotel Booking Conversation
Duration: 5:30
Sample Question - Form Completion

Complete the booking form. Write NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS AND/OR A NUMBER for each answer.

Guest Name: Sarah _______
Room Type: _______ with sea view
Check-in Date: _______ March
Special Request: Extra _______

Pro Tips
  • Read questions before audio plays
  • Listen for spelling of names and numbers
  • Watch for word limits in instructions

Reading Practice

3 Passages
True/False/Not Given Questions Reading

Determine whether statements agree with the information in the text, contradict it, or if there is no information about it.

Sample Passage (excerpt)

"The Canadian immigration system has undergone significant changes over the past decade. Express Entry, introduced in 2015, revolutionized how skilled workers apply for permanent residence. The system uses a points-based ranking called the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS)..."

Questions

1. Express Entry was implemented before 2015. (FALSE)
2. The CRS is used to rank candidates. (TRUE)
3. All provinces use the same immigration criteria. (NOT GIVEN)

Key Strategy
  • FALSE = Information contradicts the passage
  • NOT GIVEN = Information is not in the passage at all
  • Don't use your own knowledge - only use the text
Matching Headings Reading

Match the correct heading to each paragraph. This tests your ability to identify the main idea of each section.

List of Headings

i. Economic benefits of immigration
ii. Historical patterns of migration
iii. Challenges in the integration process
iv. Future trends and predictions
v. Government policy changes

Writing Practice

2 Tasks
Task 1: Letter Writing (General Training) Writing

Write a letter of at least 150 words. The letter can be formal, semi-formal, or informal depending on the situation.

Sample Task

You recently moved to a new city. Write a letter to a friend telling them about your new home. In your letter:

- Describe your new home
- Explain what you like about the area
- Invite your friend to visit you

Structure Guide
  • Opening: Dear [Name],
  • Purpose paragraph
  • Details paragraph
  • Closing: Looking forward to hearing from you
Task 2: Essay Writing Writing

Write an essay of at least 250 words. You should present a clear position and support it with reasons and examples.

Sample Task

Some people believe that immigration benefits the economy, while others think it creates challenges for local workers.

Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

40 minutes recommended 250+ words

Speaking Practice

3 Parts
Part 1: Introduction Questions Speaking

Answer questions about yourself and familiar topics. Speak naturally for about 4-5 minutes.

Sample Questions - Topic: Work

1. What kind of work do you do?
2. Why did you choose this job?
3. Do you prefer working alone or with others?
4. What would be your ideal job?

Part 2: Long Turn (Cue Card) Speaking

Speak for 1-2 minutes on a given topic. You have 1 minute to prepare.

Sample Cue Card

Describe a place you would like to visit in Canada.

You should say:
- Where it is
- How you know about this place
- What you would do there
- And explain why you want to visit this place

Part 3: Discussion Speaking

Discuss abstract ideas related to the Part 2 topic. Show your ability to analyze and express opinions.

Sample Questions - Topic: Travel & Tourism

1. How has tourism changed in recent years?
2. What are the benefits and drawbacks of tourism for local communities?
3. Do you think virtual travel experiences will replace real travel?
4. How can governments balance tourism with environmental protection?

CELPIP Practice Materials

Listening: Conversation in Context Listening

Listen to a conversation in a Canadian workplace or social setting. Answer questions about the main ideas and specific details.

Sample Question Type

You will hear a conversation between a manager and employee about a project deadline. Listen and answer:

What is the main concern of the employee?
A) The budget is too small
B) The timeline is too short
C) The team is understaffed
D) The requirements are unclear

CELPIP Advantage
  • All Canadian accents - no British/Australian confusion
  • Computer-based - type your answers
  • Results in 4-5 business days
Writing: Email Response Writing

Write an email responding to a given situation. Common topics include workplace issues, community matters, or personal situations.

Sample Task

Your neighbour's dog has been barking loudly at night, disturbing your sleep. Write an email to your neighbour:

- Explain the problem
- Describe how it affects you
- Suggest a solution

Speaking: Giving Advice Speaking

Record your response giving advice to someone in a particular situation. Speak clearly into the microphone.

Sample Prompt

A friend is thinking about moving to a new city for a job. They are unsure whether to accept the offer. Give them advice about what they should consider when making this decision.

Sample Text

Read a passage and write a one-sentence summary of 5-75 words. Tests both reading and writing skills.
